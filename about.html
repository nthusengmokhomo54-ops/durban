Discover Durban

A city rich in history, culture, and unforgettable attractions.

History & Culture

Durban’s history is deeply intertwined with the arrival of the Zulu people, the British settlers, and the Indian indentured laborers who helped shape the city’s cultural diversity. Founded in 1835 and named after Sir Benjamin D’Urban, the city has grown into a thriving port and cultural center.

The fusion of African, Indian, and European influences gives Durban a unique charm. Visitors can experience this rich heritage through its architecture, art, and festivals that celebrate unity in diversity.

“Durban’s culture is a living, breathing tapestry — woven from generations of stories, music, and flavors.”

Today, Durban is known not only for its stunning beaches and bustling markets but also for being the heart of Zulu Kingdom traditions. From the rhythmic beats of traditional dance to the aroma of spicy curries, every corner of the city tells a story.

Must-See Attractions

Durban offers something for every traveler. Adventure seekers can climb the arch of the iconic Moses Mabhida Stadium for panoramic views, while families enjoy marine life encounters at uShaka Marine World. The Golden Mile promenade invites joggers, cyclists, and beach lovers to soak up the coastal vibes.

Just outside the city, the Valley of a Thousand Hills provides breathtaking scenery and opportunities to explore authentic Zulu villages. Whether it’s surfing, sightseeing, or savoring local delicacies, Durban guarantees an unforgettable experience.

Did You Know?

Durban is one of the busiest ports in Africa and boasts a rich blend of cultures, incredible street food, and world-renowned surfing beaches.

Durban has the busiest port in Africa and is home to one of the world’s largest aquariums at uShaka Marine World!
